Water Jug Problem in AI

Problem Statement:

  • Given two jugs, one with a capacity of 4 liters and the other with a capacity of 3 liters. Both jugs have no measuring markers. The objective is to measure exactly 2 liters of water using these two jugs.

Steps to Solve:

  1. Initial State: Both jugs are empty (0,0).
  2. Action: Fill the 3-liter jug completely. State becomes (0,3).
  3. Action: Pour the water from the 3-liter jug into the 4-liter jug. State becomes (3,0).
  4. Action: Fill the 3-liter jug again. State becomes (3,3).
  5. Action: Pour water from the 3-liter jug into the 4-liter jug until the 4-liter jug is full. State becomes (4,2).
  6. Action: Empty the 4-liter jug on the ground. State becomes (0,2).
  7. Goal State: Now, the 4-liter jug contains exactly 2 liters of water.

Diagrammatic Representation:

    Start (0,0)
     |
     V
    (0,3) <-- Fill 3L jug
     |
     V
    (3,0) <-- Pour 3L into 4L jug
     |
     V
    (3,3) <-- Fill 3L jug again
     |
     V
    (4,2) <-- Pour from 3L to 4L jug until full
     |
     V
    (0,2) <-- Empty 4L jug on the ground

8-Puzzle Problem

Problem Statement:

  • Given a 3x3 board with 8 numbered tiles and one empty space, the objective is to move the tiles to achieve a specific goal configuration.

Example:

  • Initial State:
1 2 3
4 5 6
7 8 _
  • Goal state:
1 2 3
4 5 6
7 8 _

Steps:

  1. Move the blank tile (empty space) by swapping it with an adjacent tile.
  2. Continue swapping until the tiles reach the goal configuration.

Diagram:

Initial State       Move Left       Goal State
  1 2 3              1 2 3            1 2 3
  4 5 6   ----->     4 5 6   ----->   4 5 6
  7 8 _              7 _ 8            7 _ 8

Missionaries and Cannibals Problem

Problem Statement:

  • You have 3 missionaries, 3 cannibals, and a boat that can carry at most two people. The goal is to move all missionaries and cannibals across the river. However, on either side of the river, missionaries must never be outnumbered by cannibals, or they will be eaten.

Steps to Solve:

  1. Initial State: All missionaries and cannibals are on one side (MMMCCC, B -).
  2. State 1: Two cannibals cross over (MMMC, B - CC).
  3. State 2: One cannibal returns (MMMCC, B - C).
  4. State 3: Two cannibals cross again (MMM, B - CCC).
  5. State 4: One cannibal returns (MMMC, B - CC).
  6. State 5: Two missionaries cross (MC, B - MMCC).
  7. State 6: A missionary and a cannibal return (MMCC, B - MC).
  8. State 7: Two missionaries cross again (CC, B - MMMC).
  9. State 8: A cannibal returns (CCC, B - MMM).
  10. State 9: Two cannibals cross (C, B - MMMCC).
  11. State 10: One cannibal returns (CC, B - MMMC).
  12. State 11: The third cannibal crosses ( - , B - MMMCCC).

Diagram

Initial Setup: MMMCCC B -
State 1:      MMMC B CC
State 2:      MMMCC B C
...
State 11:     - B MMMCCC

8-Queens Problem

Problem Statement:

  • Place eight queens on an 8x8 chessboard such that no two queens attack each other.

Solution Approach:

  • State: The position of queens on the board.
  • Operators: Move a queen to a position on the board.
  • Goal State: All queens are placed on the board such that no queen can attack another.

Diagrammatic Representation:

  • Place each queen in a way that it does not share the same row, column, or diagonal with another queen.

Example:

  . Q . . . . . .
  . . . Q . . . .
  Q . . . . . . .
  . . . . . Q . .
  . . Q . . . . .
  . . . . . . . Q
  . . . . Q . . .
  . . . . . . Q .

Summary of Concepts

  • State: A specific configuration of the problem.
  • State Space: Collection of all possible states.
  • Initial State: The starting configuration.
  • Goal State: The desired end configuration.
  • Transition: The action that moves from one state to another.
  • Path: A sequence of states connected by transitions.
  • Search Strategy: The method used to explore the state space to reach the goal state.

Formalizing Search in a State Space

State Space

  • State Space: A graph (V, E) where V is a set of nodes and E is a set of arcs.
    • Node (V): Corresponds to a state.
      • State Description: Describes the state.
      • Optional Information: May include details related to the parent of the node, the operation to generate the node from that parent, and other bookkeeping data.
    • Arc (E): Corresponds to an applicable action or operation.
      • Source and Destination Nodes: The nodes connected by an arc are called:
        • Parent: Immediate predecessor.
        • Child: Immediate successor.
      • Ancestors: Predecessor nodes.
      • Descendants: Successor nodes.
      • Arc Cost: Each arc has a fixed, non-negative cost corresponding to the cost of the action.

Node Operations

  • Node Generation: Making a node explicit by applying an action to another node that has already been made explicit.
  • Node Expansion: Generating all children of an explicit node by applying all applicable operations to that node.

Key Elements

  • Start Nodes: One or more nodes designated as start nodes.
  • Goal Test: A predicate applied to a node to determine if its associated state is a goal state.
  • Solution: A sequence of operations associated with a path in the state space from a start node to a goal node.
  • Solution Cost: The sum of the arc costs on the solution path.

State-Space Search Process

  • State-Space Search: The process of searching through a state space for a solution by making explicit a sufficient portion of an implicit state-space graph to include a goal node.
    • Initially, V={S}, where S is the start node.
    • When S is expanded, its successors are generated, and those nodes are added to V. The associated arcs are added to E.
    • The process continues until a goal node is generated (included in V) and identified (by goal test).

Node Categories During Search

  • Not Generated Yet: The node has not been made explicit yet.
  • OPEN: The node has been generated but not expanded.
  • CLOSED: The node has been expanded.

Search Strategies

  • Search strategies differ mainly in how to select an OPEN node for expansion at each step of the search.

A General State-Space Search Algorithm

Node Structure

  • Node n:
    • State Description
    • Parent: May use a backpointer (if needed).
    • Operator: Used to generate n (optional).
    • Depth: Depth of n (optional).
    • Path Cost: Path cost from S to n (if available).

OPEN List

  • Initialization: {S}
  • Node Insertion/Removal: Depends on the specific search strategy.

CLOSED List

  • Initialization: {}
  • Organization: By backpointers.

General Algorithm

open := {S}; 
closed := {};
repeat
    n := select(open); /* select one node from open for expansion */
    if n is a goal
        then exit with success; /* delayed goal testing */
    expand(n)
    /* generate all children of n
       put these newly generated nodes in open (check duplicates)
       put n in closed (check duplicates) */
until open = {};
exit with failure
